Experience & Skills.

Graduated from the University of Melbourne with a Bachelor of Engineering (Software, with honours) and Bachelor of Commerce (Accounting) I joined the National Australia Bank (NAB) in 2007 as a technology graduate. Since that time I've worked across a variety of roles focused on web application development & content management with a short detour into business intelligence.

Through these roles I’ve gained strong experience across the SDLC and am well versed with OOP, software development practices (version control, continuous integration), stakeholder engagement and working with large enterprise teams/projects but more often within smaller, focused project teams driven by agile methodologies.

Experience across both back and front-end web development ensures I’m well rounded and versatile enough to handle anything from translating PSDs into functional HTML/CSS, MV* javascript development, integrating with content management systems or building and debugging backend APIs and database integrations.

Whilst with NAB I was proud to have been key to delivering the responsive, cross browser redesign of NAB Broker, an administration front-end utilising AngularJS and RESTful services for the NAB Flik mobile payments platform and NAB UI, an internal UI framework based on Bootstrap that has been extended to include NAB branding, custom components, and a pattern library to help our developers create responsive, consistent and compatible applications for NAB's thousands of internal users.

More recently I was part of the development team that migrated Telstra.com.au onto the AEM platform. Although visually the enhancements seemed minor, the site was rebuilt from scratch in order to make it responsive and provide a solid foundation for future development and ease of use for content producers. This was accomplished by fully utlising the platform and ensuring as many of the UI components developed were flexible and widely reusable.

For more detail and past experience, please refer to my resume or LinkedIn.

Other Tidbits.

Built from scratch using HTML5 + CSS3 and some Javascript, it’s responsive, relatively cross-browser (farewell IE8 and lesser friends) and uses CSS3 transforms/transitions to maintain animation performance. Bower handles component management (only FastClick and Normalize.css really), TypeKit for webfonts and Grunt is used for building the SCSS (using SASS and Compass) along with some other tasks such as JSHinting, concatenation and minification.

For Javscript I stuck with the plain stuff despite how awesome jQuery is for convenience, it's probably a bit much for this dinky thing.

Device mockups were done using some great free mockup templates; FlatPhone, FlatPad and FlatMac.
